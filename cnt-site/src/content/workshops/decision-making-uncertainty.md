Uncertainty is not a bug in decision-making. It is the normal condition. The question is not how to eliminate it. The question is how to move forward anyway.

## Who This Is For

Women facing decisions where the information is incomplete — career moves, educational choices for children, community involvement, business direction. If you have ever felt paralyzed by "what if," this workshop is for you.

## What You Will Learn

**Session 1: The Anatomy of a Decision**
Most people think decision-making is about choosing between options. It is not. It is about clarifying what matters. You will learn to separate values from preferences, identify what information you actually need, and recognize when you have enough to move.

**Session 2: Working With Incomplete Information**
You will never have all the data. You will learn frameworks for making sound decisions with what you have — including when to decide, when to wait, and when to gather more. Not formulas. Ways of thinking.

**Session 3: Recovering From Decisions**
Every decision creates ripples. Some you expect. Some you do not. You will learn to assess outcomes honestly without falling into regret — and to adjust course without seeing adjustment as failure.

## The Core Insight

Most people wait for certainty before deciding. But certainty almost never arrives — it is manufactured after the fact, in hindsight. The women who navigate well are not the ones who always choose correctly. They are the ones who choose clearly, learn from outcomes, and keep moving.
