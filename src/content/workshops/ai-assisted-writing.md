AI can write. But should it? And how do you use it without losing your voice? This workshop is about partnership — using AI as a thinking tool, an editor, and a collaborator, not a replacement.

## Who This Is For

Women who write — emails, reports, lesson plans, articles, WhatsApp messages to their community, content for a business. If words are part of your life, this workshop will change how you produce them.

## What You Will Learn

**Session 1: AI as a Writing Partner**
What AI does well with language. What it does poorly. The difference between generating text and refining thought. You will use AI to brainstorm, outline, and draft — and learn when to hand the reins back to yourself.

**Session 2: Finding and Keeping Your Voice**
AI is good at producing generic text. It is not good at sounding like you. You will learn techniques for teaching AI your tone and style — and for recognizing when the output has drifted from your voice.

**Session 3: Writing for Different Audiences**
How to adapt tone, length, and structure for different contexts — professional, personal, educational, communal. You will practice shifting between modes with AI assistance.

**Session 4: Editing and Refinement**
The real skill is not generating text. It is editing it. You will learn to use AI as an editor — catching repetition, tightening arguments, finding clearer ways to say what you mean. By the end, you will have a polished piece of writing and a repeatable process.

## What You Need

A laptop or tablet with internet access. Something you want to write — it can be anything from a work email to a personal essay.
