# Business Model — Change Navigation Training

## Core Insight

CNT does not sell AI training. It sells **reduced cognitive load**. Every audience segment shares this need — only the framing changes:

- **Start (18–24)**: "Walk into any room ready."
- **Build (25–35)**: "Get hours back every week."
- **Lead (35–50)**: "Lead through change on your terms."
- **Renew (50+)**: "Your wisdom, amplified."

## Revenue Architecture

| Revenue Stream | Price Point | Est. Conversion | Volume for $10K MRR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Memberships | $69–89/mo | 5% of free call → member | 125 active |
| Workshops à la carte | $35–75 | 20% of free call → attendee | 40/mo |
| Private coaching | $125–840 | 10% of free call → session | 8/mo |
| Organizational training | $500–2,500 | 1–2/mo (outreach) | 3/mo |
| Digital products | $49–199 | 2% of email list | 15/mo |

## The Math

1,000 email subscribers → 100 free Calendly calls → 20 workshop signups → 5 new members. That's $445/mo in new recurring revenue per cohort. Monthly cohorts → $5K MRR in 6 months.

## Customer Journey

```
Discover → Trust → Try → Buy → Stay
```

| Stage | Channel | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Discover** | WhatsApp groups, LinkedIn, blog SEO, speaking | They find a post or hear you speak |
| **Trust** | Blog (CNT voice), email sequence, free resources | The voice does the selling |
| **Try** | Free 20-min Calendly call | Listen. Diagnose. Invite. |
| **Buy** | $35 first workshop → membership offer | Low friction. Workshop sells membership. |
| **Stay** | Peer circles, monthly Q&As, fresh content | Community is the retention moat |

## Flywheel

Blog post → email signup → 5-email nurture → free call → $35 workshop → membership → member shares → new readers

## Competitive Positioning

CNT competes with the feeling that "AI is not for people like me."

| Conventional AI Training | CNT |
|---|---|
| Technical, male-coded, fast | Relational, women-only, unhurried |
| "Learn to code" | "Learn to navigate" |
| Career-only framing | Whole-life (work, home, community, self) |
| Secular/neutral | Frum values, Shabbos-aware |
| Individual, self-paced | Community-based, peer circles |
